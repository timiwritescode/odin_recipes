Egusi with Efo iyana ipaja Soup

Description

This recipe is a very delicious one of Yoruba (western Nigeria) origin.

The key ingredient here is the iyana ipaja vegtable which is very tasty when combined with egusi (melon).

Ingredients

  • Egusi (melon)
  • Efo iyana ipaja
  • Fresh pepper
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Palm oil
  • Iru (Locust beans)
  • Ponmo (Cow skin)
  • Smoked cat fish
  • salt
  • seasoning
  • Beef, chicken or any meat of your choice (optional)

Pre-processing

These are the necessary things to do and prepare before cooking the soup.

  • Pepper

    For the soup mix for four people, blend 12 medium sized fresh pepper, 2 medium sized tomatoes (or a big one)
    and one medium sized onion together.

  • Melon

    For amount enough for people, soak 1/2 milk cup of melon in water for 10 minutes and blend afterwards

  • The vegetable

    Pluck up to 30 medium sized efo iyana ipaja leaves, wash and dice.
    Be careful when plucking the leaves, the thorn from the tree irritates the skin!

  • Fish and Cow skin

    Cut the cow skin into little mouthful pieces and cut the smoked cat fish into small chops

Steps

  1. Pre heat your pot on the cooker for a few seconds.
  2. Put four big kitchen spoonful of palm oil in the pot and allow to bleach till the semi liquid turns complete liquid.
  3. Add three spoonful of locust beans into the bleached palm oil
  4. Pour the ground pepper into the mix, stir and cover the pot to allow the pepper steam for 5minutes.
  5. Add salt and seasoning to taste.
  6. Put the Cow skin and smoked fish into the mixture, stir and allow to steam for one minute
  7. Remove the cow skin and smoked fish into a different plate
  8. Pour the melon evenly into the steaming pepper and allow to cook for about two minutes.
  9. Stir the mixture
  10. The vegetable is ready to go in the mix. Use your hand to disperse the vegetable into the mix and stir.
  11. Add the fish and cowskin back into the soup and allow to cook together for two minutes
  12. Stir the soup one last time and turn off heat.
  13. Voila! Your soup is now ready to be served with preferrably any morsel of your choice. (Pounded yam is a big favorite here)
    And if you choose to, add your chicken or beef. bon appetite

Total cooking time is less than 30 minutes
